Marriage Process

1. Contact me today to fill out a Notice of Intended Marriage (NOIM). You can also download the form here.

2. Once the Notice of Intended Marriage (NOIM) form is filled out, it must remain with me for a period of one calendar month. During this time, I will also help you fill out all the relevant documentation and meet any identity requirements.

3. On your wedding day, I will officially solemnise your marriage in the presence of the Groom and Bride as well as two witnesses.

4. I will then apply for an Official Marriage Certificate through the Marriage Registry Department.

5. After 4 – 6 weeks, your marriage will be registered with the Marriage Registry Department, and your Official Marriage Certificate will be delivered to you by mail.

You may also read the following brochure in English here or Farsi here for further information on getting married in Australia.

You need the following documents in order to officially get married in Australia:

  • Birth Certificate
  • Driver License
  • Passport
  • Proof of cessation of marriage if you have previously been married

If you do not possess any of the above documents, you will need to fill out, and sign a Statutory Declaration with an approved witness. More information can be found at https://www.justice.vic.gov.au/statdecs.

If any of the above documents are in another language, they have to be translated to English by an approved NAATI translator. My business also offers approved NAATI translations from Dari or Farsi to English.
